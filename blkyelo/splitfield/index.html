Asite · Visual Language · Direction 05

Split Field.

Two planes, one seam. No borders, no shadows, no texture — mass meets mass and the seam does the talking. Built loud, set heavy, and carried by photography.

01 — Logo

The mark, set in mass

The Asite mark renders in a single plane colour — yellow on ink, ink on yellow. The wordmark is Inter Black at -5% tracking, sentence case: it reads as weight, not as a monument. No Cornerstone, anywhere.

Asite

The lockup sits on a plane and never floats inside a border or a rounded chip. Separation is a change of plane; emphasis is a change of mass.

02 — Color

Four planes, three signals

A colour is either there or it isn't — no tints, no washes, no gradients. Yellow is the field: claim and selection. Ink is the mass. Paper and bone are where the work lives. Signals are solid and used only as state.

03 — The seam

The only ornament, and it isn't one

A seam is where two planes meet. Give it a position and it stops being decoration and becomes a reading: progress, share, and NOW are all "how far the yellow travels." It is the brand's signature line — used for splits, dividers, and bars.

The split

Two planes meet at a 10px seam. The divide is the layout — no border draws it.

The bar

A seam given a position. The yellow travel is the data: progress, share, register health.

NOW
The now-line

On a timeline, the seam is the present moment — where poured meets planned.

04 — Typography

Weight does the talking

One family — Inter — from Light to Black. Hierarchy is mass, not size alone; headlines are Inter Black set tight and in sentence case. JetBrains Mono is stencil duty only: refs, stamps, coordinates. There is no display face.

06 — Illustration

Flat shapes, thick rods

When a photograph won't do, illustration is flat geometric shapes in plane colours — no gradients, no fine line-art. Lines, when they appear, are thick and bold: the rod and the seam doing structural work.

07 — Imagery

Photography carries the emotion

Split Field is built on good imagery more than line-art. Photographs run full-bleed and full-colour — real sites, real people, real cranes — cropped bold and framed by a plane and a seam. Type carries the argument; the picture carries the feeling.

FULL COLOURNo filters

Photos run in their actual colours. No duotone, no grayscale — the yellow plane is enough brand signal beside them.

SEAM FRAMEOne yellow edge

An image meets the layout through a single 10px yellow seam — top or side. Never a full border, never a rounded corner.

THE STAMPMono on yellow

Captions are a solid yellow stamp with ink mono — a site reference, a grid, a stage. It sits flush to an edge.

09 — Spacing & grid

8px base, poster margins

Planes breathe. Space runs on an 8px base with generous, poster-scale section margins. Layout sits on a twelve-column grid; the split is simply where the columns break.

14 — Principles

The craft contract

Six rules keep Split Field loud without becoming noisy.

Planes, not boxes

Separation is a change of plane. Nothing is contained by a border or lifted by a shadow.

The seam carries data

Every divider has a position, and the position means something — progress, share, or now.

Weight does the talking

Hierarchy is mass. Inter Black for the claim, set tight, sentence case — never wide caps.

Photography carries emotion

Lead with real, full-colour imagery. Line-art is the exception, not the rule.

A colour is there or it isn't

No tints or washes. Yellow is the field; signals are solid; everything else is ink and grey.

Zero chrome

Zero radius, zero shadow, zero texture. The page is mass, type, photograph, seam.
