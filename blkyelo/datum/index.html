01 — Logo

The mark, set quiet

The Asite mark in primary text — it behaves like type, not an object. The arrow is the only place the signal is permitted at brand level: a single yellow stroke. The wordmark is Inter 590, sentence case.

Asite

No caps, no wide tracking. The lockup reads as a product name, sitting on the grid the way a setting-out point sits on a drawing.

02 — Color

A near-black ramp, one signal in line

Four near-black surfaces separated by single-digit value steps — depth comes from 1px borders and light, not from contrast between fills. Survey Yellow is the one signal, and it appears as line-work: the datum, hairlines, ticks, focus, underlines. Never a filled block — except a single primary action per screen.

04 — Typography

Three voices, one instrument

Display is Exo 2 — a precise, technical geometric sans that reads like the lettering on an instrument face; reserved for headlines and large readouts only. Working text is Inter, Light to Semibold, at instrument density. JetBrains Mono carries every coordinate, reference, and tick — the engineering alphanumerics.

10 — Controls

Quiet, with a crosshair focus

Controls are bordered, not filled — depth from a hairline. The one rationed fill is the single primary action per screen; everything else is a yellow outline or an underline. Focus is a yellow ring, like a crosshair landing.

14 — Principles

The craft contract

Six rules keep Datum quiet and exact.

Borders & light, never fills

Separation comes from a 1px line and a value step — not from blocks of colour.

One signal, used as line

Survey Yellow is the datum, the hairline, the tick, the focus — line-work, not a field.

Measured from one line

The datum crosses everything; every reading references it.

One fill per screen

The single primary action may take the yellow fill. Nothing else does.

Figures in mono

References, coordinates, and levels are JetBrains Mono — the engineering voice.

Quiet until it matters

Status is a dot. The instrument stays calm until a value needs you.
