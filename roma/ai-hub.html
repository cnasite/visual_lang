01 — The mark

The curved spark.

Intelligence has one glyph — the setting-out cross softened into a four-point star. A thin line when it is a quiet label; filled Neo Red when it is the live agent, where it pulses. The orb sets the spark at the datum of the survey rings. Never a robot, a speech bubble, or a pair of eyes.

Quiet — a label

1.4px line. For menu items and any non-live reference to Intelligence.

Live — the agent

Filled Neo Red, pulsing. Marks the act of intelligence, once.

The orb — entry

The spark at the datum. Entry points, avatars, hero moments.

10 — Voice

How Intelligence speaks.

A competent colleague, not a chatbot. Leads with the answer; shows working only on request; cites sources; admits uncertainty plainly. No emoji, no marketing superlatives.

In register

"Rev A is blocked pending RFI-4182. The register shows Rev B as current — I can flag the mismatch. Sources attached."

Out of register

"Great question! 🎉 I'd be absolutely delighted to help you supercharge your workflow! Let me work my magic…"

11 — The discipline

The lock on the AI layer.

Do

  • Reserve Neo Red for Intelligence — the spark, the blob, the live run. The one accent in the product.
  • Match the form to the job: query bar to ask, insight to inform, Coworker to act.
  • Keep in-context insight to one line; a register row to one word.
  • Show the Coworker's steps in the open and preview every change.
  • Cite a source for every claim about project data.

Don't

  • Put a thick or coloured border on any card, panel, banner or row — no edge bars, ever.
  • Use Neo Red anywhere outside the Intelligence layer.
  • Give an insight a background, fill or pastel wash.
  • Apply a Coworker change silently, or use a filled red error banner.
  • Introduce AI purple, gradients, robots, eyes or speech-bubbles.
