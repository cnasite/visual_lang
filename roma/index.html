Asite · Visual Language — Roma

The record is the design.

A fully achromatic proof sheet — white canvas, near-black ink, a structured gray scale. Letterform precision carries the weight; the interface has no colour at all. Red survives in exactly two places: the Asite logo, and an error. Software that earns trust by never raising its voice.

01 — Logo

The mark, used verbatim.

The official Asite lockup, unmodified. Neo Red lives in the cube — it is the one place any red appears in the brand. The primary lockup leads on light surfaces; the white lockup on dark. Never redraw, recolour, or re-space the mark.

Files supplied verbatim: asite-logo-primary.png (light), asite-logo-white.png (dark), asite-logo-red-white.png (dark brand), plus cube-only crops for app rails, avatars and favicons. Neo Red is #FF3131 and appears only inside the mark.

02 — Colour

A narrow ramp, no accent.

03 — Typography

Inter, pressed into the page.

One typeface for the whole shell — Inter as the Diatype substitute — at negative tracking that compresses letterforms until display text feels engraved. JetBrains Mono keeps the engineering role: references, quantities, telemetry. Weights never exceed 600.

03b — Iconography

Site tools & functional icons.

A 24px grid, 1.5px stroke, rounded joins. Icons inherit text colour — gray at rest, ink when active — and never carry colour. The full set (90+) lives in the asset library; a sample below.

03c — Illustration

Drawn with the same pen.

Illustration is the icon grammar at scale — four grays, no colour. Line work explains; a single filled silhouette carries a brand moment. Never mix registers in one composition; the darkest fill is the anchor.

Line · elevation

Scenes from the things a site contains. Poured work solid, planned work dashed. Heroes and empty states.

Line · isometry

Data given mass — 2:1 axonometric slabs stepped through the ramp. Product moments and 3D narratives.

Filled · art

Flat silhouettes from the ramp — the darkest fill is the anchor. Covers, culture, personality.

Filled · isometry

Faces filled, outlines gone; the acted-on slab goes solid ink. App icons, launch art.

03d — Motifs

The motifs.

Each motif is a site gesture, abstracted, and rendered in ink. They never stack — pick one per composition.

The Beam

A dashed leader ending in a solid point. Direction, then arrival. For pointing at anything.

The Setting-out Cross

A decision, committed. Marks the single most important point of any artefact.

The Metre Band

Ranging-pole stripes as an edge device, in ink and white. Shared scale — covers, hoardings.

The Wash

A neutral 5% ink ground marking selection or uncertainty — attention without alarm.

04 — Surfaces & Depth

Hairlines and light, never fills.

04b — Spacing & Motion

A 4px pulse.

Every gap is a multiple of 4. Components breathe in 8s and 12s; sections in 48s and 96s. Motion is precise and never theatrical: 0.2s for colour, 0.3s for transforms, 0.4s for layout — no spring, no bounce.

04c — Cards

Four cards, one family.

All cards share the hairline border. They differ only in how they sit: flat, lifted, willing, or chosen. Hover each — it performs its own definition. The selected card takes a neutral wash and an ink ring; no card ever fills with colour.
