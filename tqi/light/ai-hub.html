01 — The layer

One instrument, now intelligent

Asite Intelligence isn't a separate product bolted on — it's the same surface, the same restraint, doing more. It reads the register, drafts the response, flags the risk, and always shows its working. The vocabulary below is shared across design and engineering so we describe the same things the same way.

Intelligence

The brand name for the layer. It is Asite Intelligence — never "the AI", "the bot", or "Copilot". A competent colleague that reads the project and acts on it.

Run

One unit of work an agent performs, start to finish. Has a run id, a state, a model, and telemetry (tokens, latency).

Tool call

A function the agent invokes mid-run — a search, a lookup, a write. Rendered as a quiet readout with its arguments and result, not a raw log.

Reasoning

The agent's working. Always opt-in and quiet — collapsed, dimmed, secondary to the answer.

Citation

A traceable source for any claim about project data — an RFI, document, or model. Required, not decorative.

Confidence

How sure the agent is, shown only when real and actionable. Low confidence always offers a path to verify.

02 — Principles

How intelligence behaves here

Six rules keep the AI layer feeling like the same trustworthy instrument rather than a different app wearing the logo.

Only the live thing glows

Orange is the only accent, and the most natural thing to carry it on an agentic surface is whatever is running or streaming right now. Queued, idle, and finished states stay monochrome and dot-first. Don't paint every step orange — and don't strip it to a lone dot either.

The answer leads, the working follows

Lead with the conclusion in plain language. Reasoning and telemetry sit beneath it, collapsed and dimmed, for those who want to check. Never make the thinking louder than the answer.

Provenance or it didn't happen

Every claim about project data carries a citation back to the source. On a construction programme, a confident answer without a source is a liability, not a feature.

Calm under load

If three agents run at once the surface still reads quiet — only active dots pulse, in one hue, nothing competes. Motion is reserved for the single live element; everything else holds still.

Honest about uncertainty

Show confidence only when it's real. Admit limits plainly and offer a way to verify. A fabricated number erodes trust faster than none at all.

No new vocabulary of colour

No "AI purple", no gradients beyond the one hero aurora. Intelligence is expressed through the existing system — the spark, the orb, the pulse — not a second brand.

03 — The mark

The spark & the orb

Intelligence has one glyph: a four-point spark — the setting-out cross, sharpened to a star. Filled and orange when it represents the live agent; a thin gray line when it's a quiet label. The orb is the spark given a field: concentric survey rings with the spark at the datum. Never invent a robot, a speech bubble, or a pair of eyes.

The spark — live

Filled orange. Marks the agent and the act of intelligence. The one place the accent belongs in this layer.

The spark — quiet

1.4px line, gray. For labels, menu items, and any non-live reference to Intelligence.

The orb

The spark at the datum of concentric rings. For entry points, avatars, and hero moments.

03c — Motifs

The intelligence motifs

Site gestures, extended to the AI layer. One per composition — they never stack.

The Pulse

The live signal. A burning point with expanding rings — used only for what is running or streaming, never for idle state.

The Constellation

Orchestration drawn as a survey network — sources and steps as nodes, the agent as the spark at the centre.

The Stream

Tokens arriving — a dashed, animated leader resolving to a single bright point. The caret is its smallest form.

The Gauge

Confidence as an instrument dial — a quiet arc with a needle. Reads at a glance, never shouts a number.

The Annotated Source

Provenance made visible — a document with the spark pinned to the exact line a claim came from.

The Shimmer

Thinking, before tokens arrive — skeleton bars on the surface ramp. Quiet, shaped like the content to come.

07 — Where it lives

Two ways Intelligence shows up

Intelligence appears in exactly two forms, and the difference matters. As a side chat you summon to ask about what's on screen — and as a Coworker that works alongside you inside a task: checking a metadata entry, reviewing a drawing, drafting a form. Both wear the same spark; both are assistive and access-scoped, never the final decision.

The side chatSUMMONED

The always-available way in — a soft-glowing orb at the bottom-right of any surface. Open it to ask about the project, the register, the document in view. It answers from what you're permitted to see and cites its sources.

  • Floating orb + "Ask Asite Intelligence" pill
  • Opens a docked conversation panel
  • Context-aware: knows the screen you're on
  • Read-only by default; proposes, you act
The in-workflow CoworkerEMBEDDED

Intelligence that does part of the job with you, inside the task itself. It reads, extracts, compares, and drafts — then hands you discrete, reviewable changes. You stay in control: nothing is applied until you say so.

  • Lives in the task: upload, form, drawing, bid
  • Runs steps and returns a checkable result
  • Field-level reconcile, never a silent overwrite
  • Re-runnable; every change is yours to apply

09 — Voice

How Intelligence speaks

A competent colleague: leads with the answer, shows working only on request, cites its sources, admits uncertainty plainly. Never role-plays enthusiasm.

Say this

"RFI-0847 is holding the L24 pour. I drafted the response and flagged D. Mensah — likely clear by Thursday. Source: RFI-0847, P-1131."

Not this

"Great question! 🎉 I'd be happy to help with that. There may be some items that could potentially be affecting your schedule…"

Honest about limits

"I can't confirm the MEP clearance from the current model — the rev is 3 days old. Want me to request the latest from Mott?"

False confidence

"Everything looks great and is fully on track! No issues detected anywhere across your project."

10 — Do & Don't

The short list

Do

  • Let the live element carry the accent — running, streaming, the primary action.
  • Lead with the answer; keep reasoning collapsed and dimmed beneath it.
  • Cite every claim about project data, back to the exact source.
  • Render tool calls as quiet mono readouts, not raw JSON.
  • Show confidence only when real; pair low confidence with a way to verify.
  • Use the spark, the orb, and the pulse for everything "AI".

Don't

  • Never put a thick coloured bar or edge on the side of a component — selection and active states are a soft intent wash, never an edge rail.
  • Paint every step, token, or tool orange — or strip it to one lonely dot.
  • Make reasoning or telemetry louder than the answer.
  • Invent a robot, eyes, a speech bubble, or "AI purple".
  • Animate everything that moves data — reserve motion for what's live.
  • Show a confidence number or citation you can't substantiate.
  • Let the agent role-play enthusiasm or apologise its way through a task.
