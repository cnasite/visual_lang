02b — Color, in the world

Where the orange comes from

The intent color isn't a brand whim — it's lifted from the site. On every project in the world, this exact orange already means one thing: a human decision, made visible. The palette's personality lives in four objects.

OWNERSHIPThe surveyor's cap

The first thing you see on site is who is responsible. Orange on a head means a person who answers for the line they set. In product: the accent marks what's yours to act on.

DECISIONThe setting-out mark

A spray cross on concrete is an instruction crews build from — intent, committed, unambiguous. In product: orange appears only where a decision happens, and exactly once.

LIVE PRECISIONThe total station beam

The instrument is gray; only the live point burns. Measurement is calm until it lands. In product: focus rings, the active row, the cursor of work — the beam, nothing else.

SHARED SCALEThe ranging pole

Orange and white in metre bands — scale everyone reads from any distance, no explanation needed. In product: the accent is a unit of meaning, repeated identically, never improvised.

03c — Illustration

Drawn with the same pen

Illustration is the icon grammar at scale — four grays, one orange decision. Four modes on two axes: Line or Filled, in elevation or isometry. Line modes explain — precision contexts, product adjacency. Filled modes feel — brand moments, covers, personality (the surveyor's cap was the first). Never mix modes in one composition.

MODE 01 — LINE ART The site, in elevation

Expressive scenes built from the things a site contains: cranes, slabs, instruments, marks. Poured work is solid; planned work is dashed; the beam connects the instrument to its point. For heroes, empty states, and storytelling.

MODE 02 — LINE ISOMETRY The register, as object

Data given mass: 2:1 axonometric slabs on a platform, faces stepped through the surface ramp, the data plane floating above. States read as material — solid is done, washed orange is held, dashed is planned. For product moments and 3D narratives.

MODE 03 — FILLED ART The site, as substance

Flat silhouettes from the surface ramp — no internal strokes, shapes carry the story. One object owns the orange, filled solid like the surveyor's cap. For covers, culture, personality moments.

MODE 04 — FILLED ISOMETRY The object, made solid

The iso grammar with faces filled and outlines gone — top, left, right stepped through the ramp; the held slab goes solid orange with darkened sides. For app icons, launch art, roadmap monuments.

One pen

1.5px strokes, rounded caps — identical to the icons. If it looks drawn by a different hand, it's off-brand.

Dashed means not yet

Future floors, planned slabs, data planes — anything that doesn't exist yet is drawn in dashes.

Orange is the live point

One decision per scene: the hook point, the held slab, the beam's landing. Never two.

Grays build, never decorate

Fills come from the four-surface ramp only. No gradients inside artwork; depth is face-shading, not light effects.

03d — Motifs

The motifs

Each motif is a site gesture, abstracted. They never stack — pick one per composition.

The Beam

A dashed leader that ends in a burning point. Direction, then arrival. For pointing at anything.

The Setting-out Cross

A decision, committed. Marks the single most important point of any artefact.

The Metre Band

Ranging-pole stripes as an edge device. Shared scale — used on covers, lanyards, hoardings.

The Wash

13% orange ground marking selection or uncertainty — attention without alarm.

04e — Tables & Lists

Registers, not spreadsheets

Rows are 44px instruments: dot, mono ref, sentence-case title, chip properties. Hover lifts the surface; selection takes the wash. No zebra stripes, no vertical rules — the grid is implied, never drawn.

Numbers right-align; units stay attached

Quantities set in mono, tabular figures, flush right — magnitudes compare down the column at a glance. The unit travels with its number ("412 m³", never a separate column). An empty cell reads "—": an instrument never leaves a dial blank.

Groups are bands, not boxes

A register groups rows with a micro-caps band — a label that passes through, like a chainage marker. Never nested tables, never indentation, never a heavier border. The band has no hover: it isn't a row, it's a reading of where you are.

04f — Data Entry

Every value has a correct field

Entry is where the product asks instead of reads — so it asks precisely. The type of the value chooses the control: text gets the sans, references and quantities get the mono, decisions get exactly the options that exist. All fields share one focus behavior: intent border plus wash ring. These are live — click in.

Text — short
Single line, sans, sentence case. For names, titles, locations — anything a human would say aloud.
Text — long
Narrative entry. Grows with content, resizes vertically only. Mono never sets prose.
Alphanumeric — reference
Engineering refs take the mono — stencil duty. Uppercase enforced on entry, tabular spacing, no autocorrect.
Float — quantity
m³
Right-aligned, tabular figures, unit fixed inside the field. The unit is part of the value, not the label.
Boolean — toggle
For states that apply immediately. One toggle, one present-tense sentence. Orange means on — nothing else here may use it.
Boolean — checkboxes
Many of many. Checks confirm a submitted choice — they don't act until the form does.
Select — one of few
Radio when every option fits on screen (≤5). All choices visible — the form never hides what it's asking.

06 — Principles

The craft contract

Four rules that keep this direction honest.

Ration the signal

Orange appears once per screen. The moment two things glow, neither is primary. Status lives in dots, selection in a 14% wash.

Depth is light, not paint

No filled panels against filled panels. A 1px inset border and a soft shadow carry all elevation. Surfaces stay within a four-step ramp.

Density is respect

AEC professionals live in registers. Tight line-heights, 13px working text, properties inline — never make an engineer scroll for what could be a column.

The keyboard is the crane

Every action has a shortcut and shows it. ⌘K reaches everything. Speed is the feature that says we understand site-time.
